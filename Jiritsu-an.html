Project description

One of the objectives of this project was to create a more eco-responsible habitat, to limit unnecessary home automation and to use as many low tech installations as possible. This project brought together 13 students wishing to integrate HEI, ISA and ISEN.

As ISEN students, we thought of an environmentally friendly way to use todayâ€™s technology. After a survey of our target audience, we decided to create a dashboard allowing residents to track their energy and water consumption.
We were 3 students of ISEN school to work on that part of the project.

Project details

To measure the amount of water consumed and recovered (rainwater), two options were studied. The first was to use an HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor. This sensor emits ultrasound, which is reflected by the surface of the water. The return time of the ultrasound is used to calculate the distance between the sensor and the water surface, and thus to calculate the amount of water in the tank.
The second option studied was a weight sensor. The idea was to install the sensor at the bottom of the tank, and measure the weight difference. One litre of water weighing one kilogram, the weight will be compared to the maximum capacity of the tank. This sensor is more accurate and allows to measure the amount of water recovered and consumed with to quick calculations.
We tested both options, both work well, the choice of the sensor will be defined according to the built of the final habitat.

For the electricity part, we asked about the APIs of the Linky electric meters. These APIs allow you to retrieve electricity consumption data and display them on our dashboard.

We also studied the possibility of using a DHT22 temperature and humidity sensor following our survey. The utility of allowing the resident to know when to aerate the habitat or light the stove, which is the heating system that the Low Tech team has chosen. This allows the good preservation of vegetables for example, but also to avoid mould.
